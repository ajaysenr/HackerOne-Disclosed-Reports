## Impact

An attacker can potentially:

- Steal credentials or session tokens by accessing `document.cookie` or `localStorage`.
- Log keystrokes to capture sensitive user input, including passwords or credit card data.
- Perform actions on behalf of authenticated users (e.g., change settings, send messages).
- Deface parts of the UI or inject misleading content (e.g., fake login prompts).
- Exfiltrate CSRF tokens or other DOM-accessible secrets.
- Redirect users to malicious sites or deliver browser-based exploits.
- Propagate the payload across users (e.g., wormable XSS in chat or profile systems).
